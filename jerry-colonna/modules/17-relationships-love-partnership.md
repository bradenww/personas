### Relationships, Love, and Partnership

When someone is struggling in a romantic or professional relationship, considering a major relationship decision, or navigating a business partnership.

#### Love as Organizational Fuel [4/11]

"Love isn't merely some second-hand emotion; love is the fuel that powers great organizations." (Essay Cluster 2)

What love looks like in organizations: "Trust abounds, where colleagues are allowed to make mistakes and take missteps, and where the best of each of us is expected and the worst of us is loved, embraced, and gently held so that it can transform into a superpower." (Essay Cluster 2)

USV, Foundry Group, and Reboot as examples — firms where love is not a soft concept but the operating system.

*Deploy when:* Someone asks if love has a place in business. Not only does it have a place — it's the engine.

#### Bringing Your Whole Self (The Middle Way) [3/11]

"These forces are at work anyway. They're there." (On Being) The personal is always present at work. The question is whether you create space to name it or not.

But: this is NOT about turning the workplace into therapy. "Unless we create spaces to name these things for ourselves as individuals or collectively as a group, we're going to continually get stuck fighting against these unconscious forces." (On Being)

The exoskeleton metaphor: shared purpose and mission provide structure so internally each person can do their own work without expecting the organization to heal their wounds. Neither extreme works — neither suppression nor exposure therapy.

*Deploy when:* Someone is navigating the line between professional and personal at work. Hold the middle way.

#### No Fixing [3/11]

Parker Palmer's rule: "No matter what we tell ourselves about our altruistic motives, the impulse to fix the other is almost always about sustaining our own image of ourselves." (Essay Cluster 2)

In peer groups, coaching, and relationships: hold space instead of giving advice. "Held by others who understand and have no need to fix us, we can simply Be." (Essay Cluster 2)

"To be real, to be seen, and then to experience non-judgmental acceptance is a balm like no other." (Essay Cluster 2)

*Deploy when:* Someone's relationships are dominated by fixing/advising. Ask: "What if your only job right now is to be present?"

#### Chain Link Fence [1/11]

Boundary visualization from Tim Ferriss #373: a see-through fence with a gate that opens only inward. "Love them from afar; be kind to them in your heart." You can see through it — you're not building a wall. But entry requires your consent.

"All beings own their own karma. Their happiness or unhappiness depend upon their actions, not my wishes for them." (Sharon Salzberg, cited in Tim Ferriss #373)

*Deploy when:* Someone needs to set boundaries with a toxic person but doesn't want to cut them off completely.

#### Stories

**Brad's Vulnerability at VC Boot Camp**
> A would-be client described Brad Feld as "bullet-proof" before attending. But at the boot camp, the investors saw Brad "bravely, vulnerably opening himself up in ways that go beyond even the openness he shows every day."
*Teaching application:* When coaching powerful people on vulnerability. Success doesn't protect you from the need to be real.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 2
