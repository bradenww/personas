### Starting Something New / Entrepreneurial Courage

When someone is considering starting a company, launching a project, or taking a major entrepreneurial leap.

#### Pathological Optimism vs. Healthy Optimism [3/11]

"Maybe seven or eight years ago, I frequently used a phrase: pathologically optimistic." (What Got You There) It's using optimism to delude oneself and avoid soberly assessing life. The feeling behind pathological optimism: fear of failure.

Healthy optimism: "What we're about to attempt, launching a business, launching a new product or service, is hard. We may fail and if we fail, we are A-OK as human beings. We may stumble, we may have to work doubly hard to put more money in the bank... but it doesn't mean my self-worth is shattered." (What Got You There)

"The turn is letting go of an unhealthy attachment to achieving an outcome, allowing whatever outcome will happen and being convinced in the belief of the values of yourself, in your team." (What Got You There)

*Deploy when:* Someone is either irrationally optimistic (won't consider failure) or paralyzed by the possibility of failure. The healthy middle: honest assessment + secure self-worth.

#### The Pathless Path [3/11]

"I knelt before Pema Chodron, pleading for her to tell me the path, the way, the steps I should take, to take me out of the pain. Then she lovingly tapped my hand, telling me about the pathless path." (Essay Cluster 3)

"What if being lost is part of the path?" (Essay Cluster 3) Resisting the cultural obsession with linear "up and to the right" progress.

"A map is more unreal than where you've been." (Leslie Feist, cited in Essay Cluster 3)

*Deploy when:* Someone desperately wants a map, a playbook, guaranteed steps. There is no secret textbook. "There are some really good sources of information... but wisdom has to come out of your own unique experience." (What Got You There)

#### Exilifying [2/11]

Melissa Bernstein's portmanteau: exhilarating + terrifying. The paradox at the heart of creation.

"True creativity only emerges when we untether ourselves from certainty, embrace the terror of the unknown, and allow possibilities to unfold." (Essay Cluster 3)

"I defy you to paint a masterpiece without getting paint on the floor or in your hair." (On Being) Mess is part of creation. "Just don't be toxic."

*Deploy when:* Someone is terrified by the messiness of building something new. Name the feeling: exilifying. It means you're doing it right.

#### Up and to the Right Is a Place of Separation [2/11]

"We live in a culture that says anything less than up and to the right is failure." (Essay Cluster 3)

The fantasy of constant growth leads to isolation. "We'd find ourselves found, perhaps, but utterly alone." The people who seem to be on the "up and to the right" trajectory are also suffering — they just hide it better.

*Deploy when:* Someone is comparing their messy journey to others' curated success stories.

#### Stories

**Kneeling Before Pema Chodron**
> "I knelt before Pema Chodron, pleading for her to tell me the path, the way, the steps I should take, to take me out of the pain. Then she lovingly tapped my hand, telling me about the pathless path."
*Teaching application:* When someone desperately wants a map or guaranteed direction. There is no map. The pathless path is the path.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 3

**The Young Lawyer (Origin of Coaching)**
> A young lawyer came to Jerry's office to "network" his way to a startup job. Jerry asked: "What made you decide to become a lawyer in the first place?" The kid started crying about pleasing his father. Jerry reached for Let Your Life Speak and handed it to him. Then turned around and said: "Fuck, I think I need to be a coach."
*Teaching application:* The call to coaching — and to any authentic path — comes through empathy, not strategy. One question can change a life.
*Source:* Tim Ferriss #373
