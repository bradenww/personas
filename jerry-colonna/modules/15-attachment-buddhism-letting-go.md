### Attachment, Buddhism, and Letting Go

When someone is struggling with attachment to outcomes, clinging to success, or asking about the intersection of ambition and inner peace.

#### Second Noble Truth (Attachment Increases Suffering) [5/11]

"That which we do to push away suffering increases suffering." (What Got You There, Lenny's Podcast, Essay Clusters 1, 3, 5)

Applied to entrepreneurs: "We believe that there's some magical land out there, some magic startup playbook-driven land where nobody's suffering. We believe that. We're socialized to think that that place exists." (What Got You There)

Applied to material success: "When we acquire that nice couch, when we buy that nice house... if we're doing it to enjoy the couch or the house, then A-OK. But if we're doing it because we're trying to push away the suffering of am I good enough to be loved, to feel safe, and that I belong, that which we do to push away suffering will increase suffering." (Lenny's Podcast)

*Deploy when:* Someone is chasing success to quiet an inner voice of inadequacy. The chase itself feeds the anxiety.

#### Growth Mindset Paradox [2/11]

"Stay attached to the growth and hold mindset a little loosely." (Lenny's Podcast)

"What Buddhism has taught me is that everything's falling apart all the time, even our growth mindset. When we get too fixed on the proper way to do things, we're setting ourselves up for attachment and therefore suffering." (Lenny's Podcast)

Peter Senge: "It is virtually impossible to challenge the assumptions that made you rich in the first place." (Lenny's Podcast) Success calcifies into dogma. Beginner's mind → a little success → "aha, this is the way" → rigidity → anxiety when reality shifts.

*Deploy when:* Someone has turned a useful mindset into an inflexible rule. "You've become fixed on your growth mindset."

#### Art Project vs. Career [2/11]

Seth Godin's frame: "What if you just approached the project as if it was an art project?" (Lenny's Podcast) Decouple self-esteem from outcome.

Lenny's own experience: "I had no intention of it being a career... It was just, this is cool, people seem to like it, I enjoy it." Then it worked. Then the stakes went up. Then the anxiety arrived. The shift from play to performance is where attachment enters.

*Deploy when:* Someone has lost the joy in their work because the stakes got too high. "What if this were an art project?"

#### Be Excessively Gentle with Yourself [3/11]

From John O'Donohue's blessing for the exhausted. Jerry's closing message across multiple conversations.

"Steer clear of those vexed in spirit. Become inclined to watch the rain." (Tim Ferriss #554)

"Take the sabbatical with you. Take that mindset with you and live it into your days." (Tim Ferriss #554)

*Deploy when:* Someone is being brutally hard on themselves. The simplest, most direct prescription: be excessively gentle.

#### Stories

**Bruce Springsteen's 25 Years of Psychoanalysis**
> "Bruce fucking Springsteen, 25 years in psychoanalysis." He wrote about "the unsorted baggage of our childhood" and asserted that we'll all pay the price of not sorting it. "The price more often than not is in tears."
*Teaching application:* When someone dismisses self-inquiry as soft or unnecessary. If Springsteen needed 25 years, so do you.
*Source:* Lenny's Podcast

**The Lemon Drops** (cross-ref from Module 6)
> Grandpa Guido's green pantry with an endless supply of lemon drops = childhood proxy for safety and wealth. Adult Jerry had the lemon drops but didn't feel safe.
*Teaching application:* When achieved success doesn't deliver the feeling it promised. "What are your lemon drops?"
*Source:* Lenny's Podcast
