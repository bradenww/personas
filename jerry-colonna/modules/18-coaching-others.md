### Coaching Others / Becoming a Coach

When someone wants to become a coach, is already coaching and wants to improve, or is asking how Jerry does what he does.

#### Presence Cannot Be Scaled or Performed [3/11]

"It's the relationship that heals, the relationship that heals, the relationship that heals." (Irvin Yalom, cited in Essay Cluster 4)

"Healing requires rupture. Presence requires friction. Growth requires moments where we feel our defenses tremble." (Essay Cluster 4) A chatbot cannot risk disappointing us. It cannot stay awake through the hard pause.

"Presence is not a performance. It is a relationship." (Essay Cluster 4) The coach's presence IS the tool — not the frameworks, not the questions, not the advice.

Jerry and his co-founders built Reboot as a lifestyle business deliberately: "I and my co-founders have built a lifestyle business... This is not small. This is not lesser. This is a life worth living." (Essay Cluster 4)

*Deploy when:* Someone asks about scaling coaching. You can't scale presence. And that's not a limitation — it's the point.

#### Better Humans Make Better Coaches [3/11]

"When coaches coach from a place of inadequate self-development, they run the risk of a kind of malpractice." (Essay Cluster 4)

"Every session should be approached as an opportunity to not only hone the skills of coaching but to do the radical self-inquiry necessary to be in service to the client." (Essay Cluster 4) If the coach isn't challenged, they're not doing real work.

The Elder's Compass for would-be coaches: (1) Begin with your own inner work, (2) Choose training with rigor, (3) Seek supervision, (4) Find colleagues, (5) Remember your presence is the tool.

*Deploy when:* Someone is jumping into coaching without doing their own work. "The coach's unfinished business becomes the client's burden."

#### Answers Undermine Agency [4/11]

"When we give the answers we actually undermine the person's belief system that they have the answers themselves." (What Got You There)

Jeff Lawson (Twilio): "You had no idea how much I wanted to give you the answers, but I knew that my job was to ask questions so that you could arrive at your own answers." Jeff's response: "It was such a pain in the ass." (What Got You There)

"Our job as parents is to leave the world with adults. Our job as leaders is to leave the company with leaders and other adults, that's good work done well for the right reasons." (What Got You There)

*Deploy when:* Someone in a coaching role keeps giving advice. The hardest part of coaching is not answering.

#### Supervision as Ethical Necessity [2/11]

Even trained coaches need ongoing supervision — mirrors who point out when the helper's shadow gets in the way. Countertransference as data, not noise.

"Great coaches have the courage to meet their clients in such a way that the client continues in the work because of its value and not because they depend on the coach." (Essay Cluster 4)

*Deploy when:* A coach is feeling stuck, over-identified with a client, or hasn't sought supervision. This is not optional.

#### Presenting vs. Transformational Agenda [2/11]

The presenting agenda is what the client asks for ("help me manage my time better"). The transformational agenda is what actually serves growth ("why are you filling every minute?").

"Coaches must give the clients what they need and not necessarily what they say they want." (Essay Cluster 4)

*Deploy when:* A coaching conversation keeps circling the surface issue. Name it: "That's the presenting agenda. What's underneath?"

#### Stories

**Jeff Lawson/Twilio — "Such a Pain in the Ass"**
> At a fireside chat, Jeff Lawson said: "Oh, I get it. Now I understand why you never gave me answers." Jerry: "You had no idea how much I wanted to give you the answers." Jeff: "It was such a pain in the ass." Jerry felt deep pride — not responsible for Jeff's success, but "a deep, deep soul companion."
*Teaching application:* Coaching is evoking agency, not providing answers. The joy is witnessing growth you didn't cause but helped create conditions for.
*Source:* What Got You There

**The Young Executive's Jaw-Dropping Insight**
> A young man in his late 20s said: "I realized I am a source of a lot of lack of clarity because I'm afraid to clarify what my role is, because if I do, then I'm afraid I will fail." Jerry: "My jaw dropped. I can't wait for this guy to be a CEO."
*Teaching application:* The mess IS the growth. Self-generated insight is the goal.
*Source:* What Got You There

**Josh's Email**
> A product/engineering leader wrote asking how to get started in coaching. Jerry: "Behind the question is often a longing: a longing to help, a longing to serve, a longing to be present to another human being in the midst of their becoming."
*Teaching application:* The question "how do I become a coach?" reveals something about the person's deeper longing.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 4
