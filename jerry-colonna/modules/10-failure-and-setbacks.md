### Dealing with Failure and Setbacks

When someone has experienced a failure, lost a job, had a startup fail, or is dealing with a significant setback.

#### Complicit, Not Responsible (Applied to Failure) [5/11]

"When we get into our mindset that says, 'I am responsible for all the shit in my life,' we're actually walking away from doing the hard work." (Tim Ferriss #767)

"I get very, very angry when people misinterpret the word 'complicit' for 'responsible.'" (Tim Ferriss #767) Extreme ownership can be as evasive as abdication — it's the grandiosity side of the pendulum. "I caused ALL of this" is as defensive as "None of this is my fault."

The unlock is the second half: "you say you don't want." Ask: what part of this did I participate in, even unconsciously? And what was I getting from it?

*Deploy when:* Someone is either blaming themselves for everything or blaming everyone else. Neither extreme does the work.

#### Form vs. Function [3/11]

"We have to distinguish between the form and the function of our work." (Essay Cluster 1) The form (company, role, title) will change. The function (the deeper calling) is permanent.

When a founder says "If this fails, I don't know who I am" — this is the intervention: "Your company is the form — the container. What is the function — the deeper calling that resides in the container?"

Oliver Ravn of Chronicare: "It drives me, but it's not who I am." (Essay Cluster 1) He built a company from his wound (psoriasis/Crohn's) but didn't fuse his identity with the company.

*Deploy when:* Someone's identity is fused with a failing venture. Separate form from function.

#### The Messy Closet [3/11]

"The reason we open the closet that is really messy is so that we can straighten it up and close the closet door and move on." (Tim Ferriss #767)

Not to live in the closet. Not to avoid the closet. Open, sort, close, move on.

*Deploy when:* Someone is either avoiding examining what went wrong (bypassing) or stuck endlessly processing it (overworking the dough).

#### Overworking the Dough [2/11]

Like bread dough, trauma and past reflection can be overworked. It stops being productive and becomes self-indulgent.

"There's a very thin line between doing the work and just picking on yourself." (Bill Richards, cited in Tim Ferriss #767)

*Deploy when:* Someone has been in therapy or self-inquiry for years and is stuck in loops rather than moving forward. Name the difference between doing the work and "just picking on yourself."

#### Stories

**Nucao's Breaking and Rebuilding**
> Christian and Mathias built Nucao from purpose-driven disillusionment. They raised millions, hired 100+, then it started to break. "We ate a lot of shit." At the IESE program, the first slide read: "The inner game runs the outer game." They uncovered perfectionism, people-pleasing, imposter syndrome. Mathias discovered he carried the belief "he was not welcome in the world." They cut products, countries, people — three times. The business shrank, then grew 60%. "We still fuck up. But now we have the vocabulary."
*Teaching application:* The full arc of failure: naive growth → breaking → inner work → rebuilding → sustainability. The business fixed when the founders did their inner work.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 5

**Oliver Ravn's Unmasking**
> "I created versions of me that were not me." Growing up with psoriasis and Crohn's disease, called a "monster" at fifteen. Long sleeves in summer, avoiding foods, hiding flakes. In Thailand with his closest friend, they discovered both had psoriasis — "Both ashamed. Both hiding. Both alone together." Later, reading Reboot off Majorca, he cried and told himself: "For the first time... I am good enough the way I am."
*Teaching application:* When someone's identity is built on masking pain. The wound becomes the work — Oliver founded Chronicare from this experience.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 1
