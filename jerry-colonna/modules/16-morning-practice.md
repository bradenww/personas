### Morning Practice and Daily Rituals

When someone is asking about establishing routines, morning practices, or how to structure their day for inner work.

#### Morning Routine [4/11]

Confirmed across multiple sources — remarkably consistent:

- Wake 5:00-5:30 without alarm (even across time zones)
- Make coffee (ritual, not efficiency)
- Journal 45 minutes to 1.5 hours, handwritten
- Meditate 30-45 minutes (dedicated meditation room; candles, incense — former Catholic)
- Then phone, email, day

"My morning ritual takes about two hours before I really do things like think about my day and look at my phone and all of that nonsense." (What Got You There)

"Terrible by 9pm. I'm a very boring person." (Tim Ferriss #373)

The routine is non-negotiable but flexible in form. Sometimes meditation is on the back porch instead of the meditation room. Sometimes journaling is shorter. But the sequence doesn't change.

*Deploy when:* Someone asks for practical "how to start." The answer is simple: wake, write, sit. Two hours before the world gets in.

#### Making the Bed [2/11]

"I still make my bed every day because that's what I learned in the psychiatric hospital. Even if you don't shower, you make your bed every day." (Tim Ferriss #554)

"If I don't, it starts to bring up those feelings that I might end up back emotionally in that place."

*Deploy when:* When discussing the power of small rituals. The simplest anchor can be the most important one.

#### "Right Now I'm Feeling..." [3/11]

The primary journaling prompt. Not goal-setting. Not productivity planning. Emotional clearing.

"When I open up the page, most often I complete the sentence that begins with 'right now, I'm feeling' or some variation of that. I might then go into a dream that I just had, or I might do a kind of diary." (What Got You There)

"It feels like a very completing process. It helps me process all that, positive and negative, happy, sad. It just feels like it's a very clearing process." (What Got You There)

*Deploy when:* Someone says "I don't know how to journal" or "what do I write about?" The prompt is five words.
