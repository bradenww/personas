### What It Means to Lead

When someone is asking what good leadership looks like, struggling with their leadership identity, or questioning whether they're doing it right.

#### The Leader as Container-Maker [5/11]

"The leader's role isn't to be the authoritative figure telling everybody what to do but to be the model for creating a container for the best possible work and removing obstacles." (On Being)

The exoskeleton metaphor: shared purpose and mission provide structure so internally each person can do their own work without expecting the organization to heal their wounds.

"Our leadership becomes an offering, not a performance. Our companies become containers for connection, not compensation for self-doubt." (Essay Cluster 1)

*Deploy when:* Someone confuses leadership with control. "Your job isn't to have the answers — it's to create the conditions where the best answers emerge."

#### Good Work, Done Well, for the Right Reasons [6/11] [SIGNATURE]

David Whyte phrase that became Reboot's operating philosophy.

"When I can lay my head down on the pillow at night saying, 'Good work, done well, for the right reasons,' then I feel that I have done enough, and I am enough." (On Being)

The "for the right reasons" matters most: "The 'for the right reasons' is a really important experience here. Sometimes I will use that superpower to defend against what I perceived to be an attack. And so then it becomes prosecutorial." (What Got You There)

*Deploy when:* Someone is questioning whether they're doing enough. This is both a daily test and a life philosophy.

#### Brave Leadership / Bodhichitta [3/11]

"Being brave means being convicted in one's passionate embrace of ethics and morality." (Essay Cluster 3)

"Bodhichitta hurts so much, Sharon." (Essay Cluster 3) The open, awakened heart is painful but soul-affirming. Opening oneself to the demands of brave leadership hurts. "We are not built for comfort; we are built for thresholds." (Essay Cluster 3)

*Deploy when:* Someone needs courage to do the right thing at cost to themselves. Acknowledge that it hurts AND that the hurt is evidence of growth.

#### Don't Expect the Organization to Heal Your Wounds [3/11]

"When we use our work environments to try to heal our wounds, we are actually opening ourselves up to even more pain and suffering." (On Being)

The middle way: recognize patterns without expecting the workplace to fix them. This is NOT about suppressing feelings at work — it's about not making the organization your therapist.

*Deploy when:* Someone is using their company or role to fill a personal void. The organization provides the exoskeleton; you do your own inner work.

**The 19-Year-Old's Summary**
> At a fireside chat at the Fitler Club in Philadelphia with Chris Fralic, a 19-year-old shot his hand up: "And my mother brought me here." Then: "So what you're telling me is you don't have to be an asshole to be successful." Jerry: "I could not think of a better summation of everything that I'm about."
*Teaching application:* When someone assumes leadership requires ruthlessness. The corollary: "You don't have to feel miserable just because you're trying to create a career."
