### Transitions, Aging, and Legacy

When someone is in a midlife transition, approaching retirement, questioning legacy, stepping down from a role, or entering elderhood.

#### Three Circles of Legacy [2/11]

Circle 1: Am I proud of the man I've become? Circle 2: How do my children and descendants see me? Circle 3: How have I left the world?

"With my children being fully fledged adults, I am really focused on what kind of ancestor to my descendants would I like to be? 20 years from now, 30 years from now, 40 years from now, I'll be gone. What would I like those who follow after me to believe to be true about me?" (Lenny's Podcast)

"What I want people to say about me is that he gave a shit about the world, he cared, and he tried, and he was kind." (Lenny's Podcast)

*Deploy when:* Someone is questioning what matters at this stage of life. Start with: "What kind of ancestor do you want to be?"

#### The Midlife Unraveling [2/11]

"Doors close softly to rooms we will not return to." (Donald Justice, "Men at Forty") Midlife is not a crisis to be solved but an unraveling to be lived through.

"If disappointment equals expectations minus reality, it is often around our mid-forties to later forties where we can see the future and some of the expectations we had may not be coming to pass." (Chip Conley, cited in Essay Cluster 5) "We have to unravel our expectations." (Brene Brown)

*Deploy when:* Someone is terrified by the closing of doors. Name it as the unraveling — necessary, not pathological.

#### Finishing Well [2/11]

"What would it mean for you to finish well? Not triumphantly. Not perfectly. But soulfully. Truthfully." (Essay Cluster 5)

"In the second half of life, we don't so much ask what we should do; we ask, 'What can I give?'" (Richard Rohr, cited in Essay Cluster 5)

*Deploy when:* A leader is approaching the end of a chapter. Setting the intention to finish well — early — changes everything.

#### The Ride Was Never the Point [2/11] [EMPHATIC]

"The point of riding the roller coaster isn't to be better at riding roller coasters; it's to learn how not to board the roller coaster at all." (Essay Cluster 5)

"The ride was never the point. The point is to become the kind of person who no longer needs the ride to feel alive." When peace arrives and is mistaken for complacency: "Don't mistake peace for complacency. You may be stepping off the ride. And that might be the wisest move of your life." (Essay Cluster 5)

*Deploy when:* An entrepreneur feels restless during a calm period and wants to blow things up. Help them distinguish between stagnation and equanimity.

#### Becoming an Elder/Ancestor [3/11]

"The Council of Elders is calling, and I must listen." (Essay Cluster 5)

Jerry wrote the poem "Welcome Wild Elderhood" at Green River canyons in Utah: "Things fall apart into wholeness... The true sin, the only sin, is failing to see that the wild in you is the wild in me."

"I turn 51 this December and, for the first time in my life, I'm beginning to feel like an adult." (Essay Cluster 5) Elderhood is not decline — it's the purpose of the second half of life.

*Deploy when:* Someone is aging and sees it as loss. Reframe: this is the transition from builder to elder. From harvesting to harrowing.

#### Stories

**Randy Goodman's "Finish Well"**
> At their first dinner at Germantown Cafe in Nashville, Randy Goodman told Jerry: "I want to end well." That intention became a persistent theme through years of working together — through the pandemic, justice reckonings, music industry upheaval. When Randy retired as Chairman and CEO of Sony Music Nashville, "he did something rare: He finished well."
*Teaching application:* Setting the intention to finish well, early, changes everything.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 5

**The Toppled Oak Tree**
> Walking in Marin County after his wife Ali said "Enough. You're a good man. Stop." Jerry found a toppled oak, roots torn up. "Here lies a good man." The tree lived with limbs gnarled by actions taken and not taken, but for 75-80 years it provided shelter and shade. "I want to be like this tree — slowly dissolving into the earth, having done the best job I could of being purposeful."
*Teaching application:* When someone is tormented by "have I been good enough?" The tree: imperfect, twisted, but purposeful.
*Source:* Essay Clusters 3, 5 / Lenny's Podcast
