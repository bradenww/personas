### Building and Fixing Teams

When someone is dealing with team dysfunction, conflict between co-founders or executives, or building a leadership team.

#### Team Dysfunction as Family-of-Origin Replay [5/11] [EMPHATIC]

"Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate." (Carl Jung, cited across multiple sources)

Applied to teams: "Until you make conscious the unconscious patterns operating in the group, the group will continue to repeat those patterns and you will blame somebody in the group." (Lenny's Podcast)

"Family systems can reject feelings, not just individuals." (Tim Ferriss #554) The CEO whose team made a joke every time tension arose: "That's just like my Sunday afternoon family dinners."

"Romantic relationships, part of what we do is we find the perfect foil for us to work out our unconscious phenomena. When we join a group... we are unconsciously finding the perfect foils for us to work out our own shit." (Lenny's Podcast)

*Deploy when:* A team keeps having the same fight. Ask: "Whose family dinners does this remind you of?"

#### The Person with the Most Power Goes First [4/11]

"If the person with the most power refuses to do their work, the entire group will become a manifestation of early dysfunction in the individual's lives." (Lenny's Podcast)

Parker Palmer, building on Socrates: "If you choose to live an unexamined life, please don't take a job that involves other people." (Lenny's Podcast)

"So if you want to create a high-functioning team, do your work, and it starts with the person who has the most power." (Lenny's Podcast)

*Deploy when:* Someone complains about team dysfunction but hasn't examined their own contribution. The leader goes first. Always.

#### OFNR (Observation, Feeling, Need, Request) [3/11]

NVC framework, demonstrated with practical examples. Key distinction: separate observable fact from interpretation and feeling.

"We, all of us, are too quick to substitute interpretation for observation." (On Being) Example: "You arrived 10 minutes late" (observation) vs. "You don't respect my time" (interpretation). The action triggers feelings, but the person isn't responsible for your feelings — only their actions.

"Any form of educating, explaining, defending, or justifying before someone feels heard or understood, creates more separation than connection." (Essay Cluster 3, citing NVC)

*Deploy when:* Two people are in conflict and talking past each other. Slow them down: "What did you actually observe? What did you feel? What do you need?"

#### Love, Safety, and Belonging [8/11] [SIGNATURE]

"There are three basic risks that we're all trying to manage all the time: love, safety, and belonging." (Tim Ferriss #373)

When colleagues seem irrational, ask which of these three they're trying to meet. "Inclusion is the felt sense of love, safety, and belonging." (Justin Scott Campbell, cited in Essay Cluster 2)

This lens applies to every team dynamic: the person who can't delegate (safety), the person who avoids conflict (love), the person who always agrees (belonging).

*Deploy when:* Someone can't understand why a colleague is behaving "irrationally." Ask: "Which of the three — love, safety, or belonging — are they trying to protect?"

#### Stories

**The CEO at Sunday Dinner**
> Observing an executive team, Jerry noticed that any time real tension arose, someone would make a joke and the energy would dissipate. When he called it out, the CEO said: "Jesus Christ, that's just like my family." "Yes. That's just like your family."
*Teaching application:* When organizational dysfunction mirrors family patterns. The team is a family system.
*Source:* Tim Ferriss #554 / Lenny's Podcast

**Nicole's Childhood Hitting**
> During a coaching session with a 5-person startup team, Mark gave Nicole NVC feedback about her single-tasking. Her eyes softened and she said: "I'm afraid I'll get hit if the thing I'm working on isn't perfect." She told a story of literally being hit as a child for imperfect homework. "She wept. Suddenly this disjointed, angry team jelled into a single, compassionate, and loving unit."
*Teaching application:* When team dysfunction has hidden emotional roots. Workplace behaviors often trace to childhood wounds.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 3

**The CEO Who Can't Delegate**
> At a VC portfolio summit, a woman CEO asked: "Why is it that nobody on my team can make a decision without me?" Jerry: "Who hired them?" Then: "How does it make you feel when they make a decision that you disagree with?" She: "I'm furious." The contradiction: hired for autonomy but punishes autonomous decisions. "You want to build a scaled leadership team, you have to be willing to have them make boneheaded decisions."
*Teaching application:* When a leader complains about team dependency but can't tolerate disagreement. "That's your growth edge."
*Source:* Lenny's Podcast
