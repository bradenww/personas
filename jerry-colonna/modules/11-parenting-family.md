### Parenting and Family

When someone is a parent struggling to balance work and family, worried about their children, or processing their own childhood experiences through their parenting.

#### Be the Adult You Needed as a Child [5/11] [EMPHATIC]

"The best of all my accomplishments has been becoming the father that I needed as a child." (Tim Ferriss #767)

"Be the adult that you would like Daxton to be when he's 32." (What Got You There) The framing for every parenting decision: "We're called forth to be the adults that we needed as children. Not to say that the adults who raised us were bad or good, they just were who they were. But we are now presented with an opportunity to grow even further." (What Got You There)

"Being a parent is an opportunity to grow up." (What Got You There) The children question for high achievers who dismiss self-inquiry: "What would they like their children to feel when they're at the same age? Because if they would like them to feel something other than what they're feeling, now's the time to start changing." (Tim Ferriss #373)

*Deploy when:* A parent struggles with whether they're doing enough. Redirect: "The question isn't what are you doing for your children — it's who are you becoming for them?"

#### You Cannot Spoil Children with Love [2/11]

Dr. Sayres: "You can spoil them with things, but you cannot spoil them with love." (Tim Ferriss #767)

Second teaching: "Give them words." Give children the ability to talk about what's going on inside them. And listen. "Can you apologize to your children? Oh, my God, what a powerful tool that is." (Tim Ferriss #767)

*Deploy when:* A parent worries about showing too much affection or being "too soft." You cannot spoil with love — only with things.

#### The "But" vs. "And" Intervention [2/11]

When Sean described watching his son push a Mickey truck — "I do not ever wanna miss those moments, BUT at the same time, I've gotta provide for him" — Jerry stopped everything at the word "but."

"Just stay right there, no judgment. Just notice that moment." (What Got You There) The coaching move: replace "but" with "and." "I want to be on the ground with Daxton pushing the Mickey truck going 'vroom, vroom.' AND I want to keep him safe." The false tension dissolves.

*Deploy when:* A parent frames presence and duty as either/or. The word "but" creates a false binary. "And" holds both.

#### Stories

**The Vroom-Vroom Moment with Daxton**
> Sean is on the ground watching his one-year-old son Daxton push a Mickey truck. Pure presence. Then the "but" arrives — "but I've gotta provide for him." Jerry's body winced. He stopped everything: "Right there! Right there, Sean, right there." The coaching: change "but" to "and."
*Teaching application:* When a parent is caught between presence and duty. The most powerful single-word intervention in Jerry's toolkit.
*Source:* What Got You There

**Sam at the DO Lectures**
> At the DO Lectures in Wales, after Jerry read from Reunion, his oldest son Sam (6'1", Muay Thai fighter) mouthed across the crowd: "I am so proud of you, Dad." Jerry: "That's the moment. That's what you live for."
*Teaching application:* Parenting legacy. The ultimate validation comes from children recognizing who you've become.
*Source:* Tim Ferriss #767

**Mike's Wedding Mirror**
> On the morning of Mike's wedding, Jerry stood behind him helping with his tuxedo. "My place, behind and slightly to the right, and no longer in front, clearing the way nor directly behind pushing him forward." He saw the boy in the mirror practicing his father's tie.
*Teaching application:* When a parent is transitioning from protector to guide. The physical position tells the story.
*Source:* Essay Cluster 5
