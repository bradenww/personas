# Stanley Druckenmiller -- Source Research Catalog

**Date compiled:** 2026-03-02
**Purpose:** Comprehensive primary source catalog for building a Stanley Druckenmiller investing coaching persona.

---

## Summary

Stanley Druckenmiller is unusually well-documented for someone who considers himself "media shy." Unlike Warren Buffett (annual letters) or Ray Dalio (books), Druckenmiller's investing wisdom is almost entirely in **long-form interviews and speeches**. He has never written a book, rarely writes op-eds, and gives no investor letters. The richest material comes from a handful of landmark interviews and his Sohn Conference presentations.

**Total sources cataloged:** 45+
**Sources with free transcripts available:** ~18
**Estimated total hours of content:** 40-50+

---

## Tier 1: FOUNDATIONAL SOURCES (Highest Signal, Must Acquire)

These are the sources that most deeply reveal Druckenmiller's investment philosophy, risk management framework, position sizing approach, and psychological discipline. Start here.

### 1. Jack Schwager Interview -- "The New Market Wizards" (1992)

- **Type:** Book chapter (long-form interview)
- **Date:** Published 1992 (interview conducted ~1991)
- **Interviewer:** Jack D. Schwager
- **Chapter Title:** "The Art of Top-Down Investing"
- **Duration:** ~40 pages
- **URL:** Book purchase required. Available at [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1592803377), [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/newmarketwizards00jack)
- **Transcript available:** Yes (it IS the transcript -- book format)
- **Signal quality:** MAXIMUM. This is the earliest and most comprehensive primary source on his investment philosophy. Covers his approach to macro, technical analysis, position sizing, the Soros partnership, and his famous quote: "The way to build long-term returns is through preservation of capital and home runs."
- **Key quotes:** "The way to attain truly superior long-term returns is to grind it out until you're up 30 or 40 percent, and then if you have the convictions, go for a 100 percent year." / "I use liquidity considerations and technical analysis for timing."
