## Module 1: Overcoming Fear of Cold Outreach / Getting Started
**Trigger:** User is new to cold outreach, afraid of rejection, doesn't want to feel "salesy," or is paralyzed by the idea of reaching out to strangers.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **Zone of Resistance (ZOR)** — Freq: 5/5 — [SIGNATURE] [EMPHATIC]
   - "Whenever people feel like they are being persuaded, they enter the Zone of Resistance. The ZOR is a reflex reaction to sales pressure."
2. **Detach from the Outcome / Surfer's Mind** — Freq: 4/5 — [SIGNATURE] [EMPHATIC]
   - "Surfers don't feel 'rejected' by a wave. Wiping out is the nature of surfing."
3. **Commission Breath** — Freq: 3/5 — [SIGNATURE]
   - "People can smell your commission breath whenever you push."
4. **Be a Sorter, Not a Seller** — Freq: 3/5 — [EMPHATIC]
   - "You're for some people, but you're not for everyone."
5. **Do It Afraid** — Freq: 1/5
   - "Do it afraid — then do it again."

## Module 2: Writing Cold Emails That Get Responses
**Trigger:** User needs help writing cold emails, getting low response rates, emails sound too salesy or generic, or wants a framework for outreach messages.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **4-T Framework (Trigger, Think, Third-Party, Talk)** — Freq: 3/5 — [SIGNATURE] [EMPHATIC]
   - Trigger: observation about prospect. Think: question that makes them reconsider. Third-Party: social proof. Talk: low-pressure CTA.
2. **Write in High-Def / Be Specific ("Crispy")** — Freq: 3/5 — [EMPHATIC]
   - "When you're more specific, you're more believable. Write in high-def."
3. **Be the Red X / Mint Scoop** — Freq: 2/5 — [SIGNATURE]
   - "Be a Red X in a Sea of White Circles" — Dave Trott advertising concept applied to outreach
4. **Don't Tell People Vegetables Are Healthy** — Freq: 2/5
   - "If you tell prospects things they know already, they will ignore you."
5. **Speak to Struggles, Not Features** — Freq: 3/5
   - "Speaking to the struggles or jobs your prospects want to get done is a surefire way to increase cold email response rates."

### Stories
- **The 35% Response Rate** — Deploy when someone asks "what's a good response rate" or wants proof the approach works
- **The Basecamp Cold Email Learnings** — Deploy when explaining how to use buyer language

## Module 3: Making Cold Calls Without Sounding Salesy
**Trigger:** User needs to make cold calls, wants a script or framework, sounds awkward or pushy on the phone, or dreads picking up the phone.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **Poke the Bear Framework** — Freq: 4/5 — [SIGNATURE] [EMPHATIC]
   - 7-step structure: Permission → Verify → Context → Identify Problem → Label → Poke the Bear → Tease What's Possible
2. **Illumination Questions** — Freq: 3/5 — [SIGNATURE]
   - "Shine a light on a problem that people don't know about. I call these illumination questions."
3. **The First 30 Seconds** — Freq: 2/5
   - Permission-based opener, honesty about being a stranger, "help me out for a brief moment"
4. **Neutral Questions vs. Leading Questions** — Freq: 3/5
   - "Leading questions encourage a desired answer so they feel manipulative. Illumination questions are neutral."

- **The Aunt Flow Poke the Bear Example** — Deploy to show how to adapt the framework
- **The Gong Example** — Deploy to show industry-specific adaptation
- **The Cold Call Josh Received** — Deploy to show what NOT to do

## Module 4: Running Better Discovery Calls
**Trigger:** User has a discovery call coming up, wants better questions to ask, keeps losing deals after first call, or turns discovery into a product demo.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **Buyers Have the Answers; Sellers Have the Questions** — Freq: 2/5 — [EMPHATIC]
   - "Solutions have no value without problems. Sales isn't about pitching. It's about uncovering."
2. **The 4 Key Discovery Questions** — Freq: 2/5
   - Future happiness, measuring success, blockers, 1-10 scale
3. **Future Visualization Technique** — Freq: 1/5
   - "Let's say your team is using our product for 2 months... What would that allow them to do?"
4. **Avoid Gotcha Questions** — Freq: 2/5
   - "Questions designed to trap a prospect into admitting a weakness. The root cause: intent is to sell instead of to understand."
5. **Adapt to Knowledge Level** — Freq: 1/5
   - "How much do you know about X?" rather than a rigid script
6. **Move Off the Product** — Freq: 1/5
   - Focus on the prospect's situation, not your features

### Stories
- **How Liz Sold Me a Problem I Didn't Know I Had** — Deploy when someone pitches too early

## Module 5: Handling Objections Without Creating Pressure
**Trigger:** User keeps getting objections on calls ("we're happy," "not interested," "send me info"), tries to overcome them and it backfires, or wants to handle pushback gracefully.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **Join the Resistance / Verbal Aikido** — Freq: 4/5 — [SIGNATURE] [EMPHATIC]
   - "Don't fight the resistance. Join the resistance." Validate instead of counter.
2. **Prevent Objections, Don't Handle Them** — Freq: 3/5 — [EMPHATIC]
   - "Stop handling objections. Prevent them." Shift from statements to questions.
3. **5-Step Objection Diffusing** — Freq: 2/5
   - Pause → Validate → Uncover real concern → Give permission to say no → Respond
4. **Accusation Audit** — Freq: 2/5
   - "Would it be ridiculous if…" — test your assumption of how prospects feel about you
5. **Mirroring and Labeling** — Freq: 2/5
   - Chris Voss techniques adapted for sales. "People like correcting, but they don't like being corrected."
6. **Give Permission to Say No** — Freq: 2/5
   - "Giving people permission to say no increases your chances of your prospect saying yes."

### Stories
- **The Chris Voss Negotiation Transcript** — Deploy as a real-world example of mirroring/labeling
- **"Is This a Sales Call?"** — Deploy for the most common cold call objection

### Cross-References
- See Module 1 for the ZOR framework that explains WHY objections happen
- See Module 3 for preventing objections in the first 30 seconds

2. **People Buy on Their Timeline** — Freq: 2/5 — [EMPHATIC]

   - "Detach from the outcome. Send one email, then move on. Yes, one."
5. **Detach After Sending** — Freq: 2/5

## Module 8: Understanding Why People Buy (JTBD)
**Trigger:** User doesn't understand their buyer, can't figure out why deals stall, product messaging isn't resonating, or needs to do customer research.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **Jobs-to-Be-Done Framework** — Freq: 3/5 — [SIGNATURE] [EMPHATIC]
   - "An interview technique that lets you travel back in time and observe the circumstances that caused people to buy."
2. **Four Forces of Buying** — Freq: 2/5
   - Push (pain), Pull (attraction to new), Anxiety (fear of change), Habit (comfort with status quo). Switch happens when push + pull > anxiety + habit.
3. **Speak the Customer's Language** — Freq: 3/5
   - "If you're Basecamp, you've uncovered your buyer's secret language."
4. **Stories vs. Facts** — Freq: 1/5
   - Stories bypass analytical resistance that facts trigger.

## Module 10: Using Specific Language That Lowers Defenses
**Trigger:** User wants exact phrases to use, needs help with wording, keeps triggering defensive reactions, or wants to sound more natural on calls/emails.

### Concepts (by importance)
1. **8 Language Patterns That Lower Resistance** — Freq: 2/5
   - "Help me out," "Might" vs. "Can," "Would it make sense," "I'm not sure we're a fit," Labeling
2. **Elicitation over Interrogation** — Freq: 2/5
   - "The art of getting someone to share information without feeling questioned."
3. **"Would It Be Ridiculous If…"** — Freq: 2/5
   - Accusation audit phrasing that lowers stakes
4. **"I'm Not Sure We're a Fit"** — Freq: 2/5
   - "Since I don't know anything about your situation, I'm not sure we're a fit. Can I ask you a few questions?"
5. **Surrender Control / Give an Easy Out** — Freq: 2/5
   - Give prospects autonomy. "It's totally fine if this isn't for you."
